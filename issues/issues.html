Issues We Face

The reason that we don’t believe in gun control laws is primarily that they seem to be ineffective, regardless of how strict states make them. It should be made known that if citizens gave up their right to bear arms, the government would have the opportunity to turn against the citizens by taking away all guns or causing government tyranny. Another reason we don’t believe in gun control is that we found that it’s not the gun control laws that deter crime, it’s the gun ownership that deters crime. We found that gun control laws can’t, don’t, and won't do anything at all. Going to our point before, gun ownership had dropped violent crime rates much more dramatically than gun control laws.

We see that current gun control laws are targeted at poor, black communities because these laws were founded on racial fears. The Statera Party is strongly against discrimination of any kind. We believe that taking away some guns is an infringement of the second amendment and self-defense, as well as denying a sense of safety to U.S. citizens. It would be unnecessary to impose gun control laws when they only make things worse in terms of freedom and safety. The Statera Party is opposing gun control because it conflicts with the freedom to bear arms and self-defense.

Nothing to be taken away as (almost) everything is how we feel is right. Smoking should still be forbidden at schools, no-smoking areas in public spaces, and workplaces. The Statera Party believes in progressive taxes. Progressive taxes are taxes that progressively get higher as a person’s income gets higher. The rich pay more while the poor pay less. That way, this economy can favor the poor and prevent homelessness. Poverty is normally caused by the inability to access productive resources and high costs.

With this form of taxes, we can solve the problem of poverty much easier by allowing the poor some breathing room to get back on their feet. This can allow for inequalities in the economy to be a lot less of a problem than before. This can prevent the rich from wastefully spending their money and allow the poor to buy more necessities. The money that the rich spend on taxes can be put towards welfare and public services to be of equal and greater value for everyone. The revenue can also be put towards defences and emergency services to help make this country safer.
